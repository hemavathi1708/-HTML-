Brihadeeswarar Temple

The Brihadeeswarar Temple, also known as Tanjore Big Temple or Periya Kovil, is a 1,000-year-old UNESCO World Heritage site in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu.

Namsan Tower

N Seoul Tower (Namsan Tower) is an iconic 480-meter-high communication and observation tower atop Namsan Mountain in Seoul, offering 360-degree city views, a famous "love lock" terrace, and restaurants.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, commissioned in 1632 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls is a world-renowned trio of massive waterfalls—Horseshoe Falls, American Falls, and Bridal Veil Falls—straddling the border of Ontario, Canada, and New York, USA.

Bukchon Hanok Village

This village is famous for its beautifully preserved traditional Korean homes known as hanoks, some of which date back over 600 years to the Joseon Dynasty. It is located between the two major palaces of Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung.

Red Fort

The Red Fort (Lal Qila) in Delhi is a 17th-century UNESCO World Heritage site, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as the palace fort of his capital, Shahjahanabad.

ZhangJiaJia National Park

hangjiajie National Forest Park, located in Hunan Province, is a renowned UNESCO World Heritage site famous for its thousands of towering quartz-sandstone pillars, which inspired the "Hallelujah Mountains" in Avatar.

India Gate

India Gate, located in the heart of New Delhi, is a 42-meter-high, Sir Edwin Lutyens-designed war memorial arch dedicated to 70,000 Indian soldiers who died in World War I and the Afghan War.
